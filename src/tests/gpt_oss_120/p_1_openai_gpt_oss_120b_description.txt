**1. Setting Name**  
**The Emberforge Bastion** – a forgotten dwarven citadel hewn into the heart of the Blackfire Mountain, now a molten‑scarred labyrinth of stone, steel, and smoldering ruin.

---

**2. Visual Style & Atmosphere**  

*Art Direction – Gritty Sub‑Terranean Industrial*  
- **Geometry:** Every hall and chamber is cut with the dwarves’ signature precision: sharp, right‑angled walls, massive rectangular vaults, and monolithic stone pillars that rise like the ribs of a buried beast.  
- **Materials:** Rough basalt and iron‑rich ore dominate the palette, interspersed with veins of glowing magma‑glass and tarnished copper. Rusted gears, pistons, and ancient steam‑driven contraptions protrude from the walls, their silhouettes stark against the stone.  
- **Lighting:** A perpetual, low‑key glow emanates from flowing rivers of molten lava that snake through the floor, casting flickering amber and crimson light across the corridors. Occasional sputtering lanterns and the occasional burst of steam provide brief, harsh highlights.  
- **Atmosphere:** The air is thick with heat, the scent of sulphur, and a fine, metallic ash that settles on every surface. The constant roar of bubbling lava and the clank of ancient machinery echo through the halls, punctuated by distant, guttural chittering of goblin patrols.  
- **Mood:** Oppressive and claustrophobic in the tight mining tunnels, yet awe‑inspiring in the grand chambers where vaulted ceilings soar above molten rivers. The contrast between the crushing weight of stone and the fierce, living fire creates a sense of both dread and reverence.

---

**3. Lore**  

Millennia ago, the dwarven kingdom of **Kharaz‑Dûm** forged the Emberforge Bastion as the ultimate stronghold for their legendary **Molten Forge**—a network of foundries capable of shaping weapons from living lava. The citadel was a marvel of engineering: massive anvils fed directly by magma, storage vaults lined with rune‑etched steel, and a throne hall where the High King presided over a council of master smiths.

A cataclysmic eruption, known in dwarven chronicles as **The Ashen Sundering**, ruptured the mountain’s core. The eruption flooded the lower levels with lava, shattered the central power crystal that regulated the forge’s heat, and left the citadel sealed beneath a crust of cooled basalt. The dwarves, unable to repair the damage, abandoned the stronghold, sealing its gates and leaving only whispered legends of its riches.

Centuries later, goblin warbands, drawn by the heat and the promise of untapped metal, infiltrated the sealed passages. They repurposed the dormant machinery, using the lingering magma to power crude forges of their own. Over time, the goblins mutated under the constant exposure to molten fumes, becoming larger, more aggressive, and fiercely territorial. Their presence awakened dormant **Aetheric Sentinels**—ancient, semi‑sentient constructs left by the dwarves to guard the forge. Corrupted by the goblin infestation and the uncontrolled lava, these sentinels now roam the halls, their once‑noble purpose twisted into relentless aggression.

The Emberforge Bastion now stands as a tomb of dwarven grandeur, a furnace of perpetual danger, and a lure for any adventurer daring enough to claim its forgotten treasures.

---

**4. Core Conflict**  

The heart of the Bastion is **the Corrupted Core**, a massive, semi‑molten crystal that once regulated the flow of lava and powered the dwarven forges. The crystal has been shattered and fused with goblin‑crafted mechanisms, creating an unstable feedback loop that spews erratic bursts of lava and corrupts the surrounding aetheric energy.

- **Primary Threat:** The **Aetheric Sentinels**—once noble guardians—are now warped by the Core’s chaotic energy. They patrol the main corridors, their stone bodies humming with volatile magma, attacking any intruder with crushing force and searing blasts.  
- **Secondary Threat:** Goblin warlords and their mutated hordes have claimed the functional areas (foundries, storage rooms, throne hall) as strongholds, defending them with improvised traps, rigged steam vents, and swarms of goblin minions.  
- **Environmental Hazard:** Rivers of molten lava act as both barrier and hazard. Bridges are precarious, often collapsing under the weight of the unwary, while hidden mining tunnels provide risky shortcuts that may lead to dead‑ends, collapsing shafts, or secret chambers still guarded by dormant dwarven defenses.  

The player’s objective is to navigate this labyrinthine stronghold, survive the relentless onslaught of corrupted guardians, and ultimately **re‑stabilize or shatter the Corrupted Core**, deciding whether to restore the ancient dwarven forge, claim its power, or seal it forever. The outcome will echo through the mountain, determining whether the Emberforge Bastion becomes a beacon of reclaimed dwarven legacy or a permanent furnace of ruin.
